# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Campaign operators and hackathon judges use Hugo from a desktop workspace to launch creator campaigns, review autonomous-agent decisions, approve spending, inspect content quality, release payouts, and verify that every run improves future strategy. They work quickly across financial, operational, and learning evidence and need the system to remain legible under pressure.

## Product Purpose

Hugo makes an autonomous creator-marketing agent observable and controllable. It brings campaign state, approvals, creator progress, Stripe money movement, NVIDIA QA, and PostgreSQL learning into one auditable interface. Success means an operator can understand current risk, identify the next required action, and prove the complete earn–spend–pay–learn loop without reading backend logs.

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, and intelligent. Hugo should feel technically advanced without becoming theatrical, and polished without hiding operational detail. Copy is direct, specific, and confident.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards made from identical metric cards.
- Dark “control room” interfaces with neon glows or decorative telemetry.
- Glassmorphism, large gradients, oversized rounded containers, and ornamental motion.
- Marketing language inside operational views.
- Interfaces that hide critical state behind hover, color alone, or ambiguous icons.

## Design Principles

1. **Decisions first** — Surface the next consequential action and the evidence required to take it.
2. **Prove the system** — Make agent work, payment gates, QA, and learning observable through real state and data.
3. **Progressive expertise** — Keep the default view scannable while preserving drill-down detail for expert operators.
4. **Calm under pressure** — Use predictable navigation, stable layouts, and restrained motion so urgent states remain clear.
5. **Truth over theater** — Prefer authoritative data, explicit statuses, and honest empty/error states over decorative futurism.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG 2.2 AA. Support complete keyboard navigation, visible focus, screen-reader labels, non-color status cues, 44px touch targets, 200% zoom, responsive layouts from 375px upward, and `prefers-reduced-motion`. Charts require textual summaries and tabular alternatives.
